<span id="led-control"></span><h1>Operation - Make it Shine<a class="headerlink" href="#operation-make-it-shine" title="Permalink to this headline">#</a></h1>
<p>This section describes how control the LEDs on your Duckiebot.</p>
<p>Duckiebots have four LEDs, positioned similarly to the head and tail lights on a car.</p>
<div class="figure align-default" id="leds-layout">
<img alt="../../_images/leds_layout.png" src="../../_images/leds_layout.png" />
<p class="caption"><span class="caption-number">Fig. 46 </span><span class="caption-text">A duckiebot with the LEDs shining white and a diagram with arrows indicating the front and rear LEDs.</span><a class="headerlink" href="#leds-layout" title="Permalink to this image">#</a></p>
</div>
<p>LEDs as actuators on a Duckiebot can be used for many purposes, including</p>
<ul class="simple">
<li><p>Indicating what mode or mission the Duckiebot is running</p></li>
<li><p>Communicating state changes in the controller</p></li>
<li><p>Signaling upcoming turns or other navigation plans</p></li>
<li><p>Expressing character and personality</p></li>
<li><p>And simply lighting the driving environment</p></li>
</ul>
<div class="section" id="make-it-shine-shell">
<span id="id1"></span><h2>LED control<a class="headerlink" href="#make-it-shine-shell" title="Permalink to this headline">#</a></h2>
<p>You can update the LEDs on your Duckiebot manually by bringing up the LED Widget using the <code class="docutils literal notranslate"><span class="pre">led_control</span></code> command provided by the Duckietown Shell.</p>
<p>Open a terminal and run:</p>
<div class="highlight-none notranslate"><div class="highlight"><pre><span></span>dts duckiebot led_control ![DUCKIEBOT_NAME]
</pre></div>
</div>
<div class="admonition attention">
<p class="admonition-title">Attention</p>
<p>For all operation commands that use the Duckiebot’s name - replace <code class="docutils literal notranslate"><span class="pre">![DUCKIEBOT_NAME]</span></code> with just the Duckiebot’s <code class="docutils literal notranslate"><span class="pre">hostname</span></code>, do not include <code class="docutils literal notranslate"><span class="pre">.local</span></code> part that you used previously to access the dashboard.</p>
</div>
<p>After startup, the <code class="docutils literal notranslate"><span class="pre">led_control</span></code> command will open an interface window. Make sure the window is active by selecting it, and press the buttons to update the color and intensity of your Duckiebot’s LEDs.</p>
